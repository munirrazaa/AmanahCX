🔐Role Hierarchy Overview

AmanahCX uses a numeric rank system. Higher rank = broader access. RLS (Row-Level Security) in Postgres enforces tenant isolation at the database level — no role can read another tenant's data.

🏢

Tenant Admin

Rank 40 — Highest tenant-level role

Owns and configures the entire tenant workspace. Can hard-delete closed tickets (irreversible). Cannot access other tenants or platform settings.

  • ✓Create, view, edit all tickets in tenant
  • ✓Hard delete tickets (closed tickets only)
  • ✓Manage agents, managers, viewers — create/edit/deactivate
  • ✓Configure SLA policies, queues, routing rules
  • ✓View all audit logs across the tenant
  • ✓Access all reports and analytics
  • ✓Manage contacts (create, merge, delete)
  • ✓Configure voice bot settings and intent mappings
  • ~Change ticket priority — requires written governance reason
  • ✗Access other tenants' data
  • ✗Platform-level system settings (super_admin only)
  • ✗Delete open or in-progress tickets
🤖

Voice Bot

Rank 20 (service account) — System actor

An automated service account that interacts via API only. Accepts inbound calls, detects intent, creates tickets and contacts, then hands off to human agents. No UI access.

  • ✓Accept inbound calls and detect customer intent
  • ✓Create new tickets with detected intent as subject
  • ✓Create contacts if phone/NIC not found
  • ✓Update existing contacts (add ticket reference)
  • ✓Set initial ticket priority and queue based on intent
  • ✓Trigger automatic routing to ACD queue
  • ✓Write to ticket audit log (bot action)
  • RRead routing rules and queue configurations
  • ✗Edit or close tickets after hand-off
  • ✗View reports or analytics dashboards
  • ✗Manage users or roles
  • ✗Delete any record
👔

Manager

Rank 30 — Supervises agents and queues

Operational supervisor. Can edit all fields on any ticket at any stage, reassign agents in emergencies, and change SLA priority with a mandatory written reason.

  • ✓View all tickets in assigned queues
  • ✓Edit ticket fields (status, content, notes)
  • ✓Change ticket assignee (emergency reroute, optional reason)
  • ~Change ticket priority — mandatory written reason required (SLA governance)
  • ✓View full audit log for tickets they supervise
  • ✓Access reports scoped to their queues
  • ✓Add internal notes to any ticket
  • RView contacts (read-only unless tenant_admin grants write)
  • ✗Delete tickets
  • ✗Create or deactivate user accounts
  • ✗Modify SLA policy or routing rules
📋

Line Manager

Rank 30 variant — Team-scoped supervisor

Same rank as Manager but scoped to a specific team or queue. Oversees day-to-day agent performance within their team only. Cannot cross teams.

  • ✓View all tickets within their assigned team/queue
  • ✓Edit ticket content and notes for their team's tickets
  • ✓Reassign tickets within their team (optional reason)
  • ~Change priority — mandatory reason required
  • ✓View audit logs for tickets in their team
  • ✓Add internal notes
  • RView team performance metrics
  • ✗View or touch tickets outside their team
  • ✗Delete tickets
  • ✗Modify routing rules or SLA policies
  • ✗Manage user accounts
🎧

Agent

Rank 20 — Frontline support staff

Handles customer tickets assigned to them. TAT clock starts when the agent accepts a ticket. Can update status, add notes, and resolve. Limited to tickets they own.

  • ✓View tickets assigned to them
  • ✓Accept assigned tickets (TAT starts on acceptance)
  • ✓Update ticket status (open → in-progress → resolved)
  • ✓Add public replies and internal notes
  • ✓View contact information for their tickets
  • ownEdit ticket fields — own tickets only
  • RView audit log for their own tickets
  • ✗View other agents' tickets
  • ✗Change ticket priority
  • ✗Reassign tickets to other agents
  • ✗Delete any record
  • ✗Access reports or analytics
👁️

Viewer

Rank 10 — Read-only observer

Read-only access, typically granted to originators (customers/requestors who raised a ticket) and auditors. Can add notes on tickets they originated. Cannot change any status or field.

  • RView tickets they are linked to as originator
  • ✓Add notes/comments to their own tickets (originator only)
  • RView ticket status and history (no internal notes)
  • ✗Edit any ticket field
  • ✗Change ticket status, priority, or assignee
  • ✗View other users' tickets
  • ✗Access internal audit logs
  • ✗Delete any record
  • ✗Manage users or settings
  • ✗Access reports or analytics

🔄End-to-End Information Flow

From voice bot accepting a customer call through to ticket resolution. This covers every actor, data point created, and system event in sequence.

1

Customer Calls In — Voice Bot Accepts

A customer dials the tenant's inbound number. The telephony layer (e.g. Twilio / SIP) routes the call to AmanahCX. The Voice Bot service account answers and begins recording the interaction.

Actor
Voice Bot (service account, rank 20)
Channel
Inbound voice call (PSTN / SIP)
System event
call_started logged with caller CLI (phone number) and timestamp
Data captured
Caller phone number, call timestamp, tenant routing number dialled
2

Intent Detection

The voice bot plays an IVR greeting and listens to the customer's spoken request. Natural language processing (NLP) classifies the intent into a pre-configured category (e.g. "Billing Query", "Technical Issue", "Account Management", "Complaint").

Intent output
intent_code + confidence_score + raw_transcript
Fallback
Low confidence → routed to "General" queue for human triage
Priority mapping
Intent → default priority (e.g. "Complaint" → high, "Billing" → medium)
Queue mapping
Intent → target queue (configured by tenant_admin in routing rules)
3

Contact Lookup or Creation

The voice bot searches for an existing contact record using the caller's phone number (CLI). Three outcomes are possible:

Outcome A — Found
Existing contact retrieved. Bot reads name and prior ticket count. No record created.
Outcome B — Partial match
Phone found but NIC/name mismatches. Flagged for agent review. Existing record linked but not overwritten.
Outcome C — New caller
No match found. Bot creates new Contact record with phone + any captured name/NIC from IVR prompts.
contact_id generated Searchable by: phone · NIC · name · email
4

Ticket Creation

The voice bot creates a new ticket linked to the contact. All fields are populated automatically from the intent detection output and tenant routing configuration.

Ticket ID
Auto-generated (e.g. TKT-00042)
Subject
Derived from intent label (e.g. "Billing Query — Inbound Call")
Priority
Set from intent→priority mapping
Queue
Set from intent→queue routing rule
Status
open (pending agent acceptance)
Contact linked
contact_id from Step 3
Originator
Voice bot service account (auto)
Audit entry
ticket_created logged with bot actor

The contact record is also updated: the new ticket_id is appended to the contact's ticket history, giving agents a full call/ticket timeline.

5

Automatic Routing to Agent Queue

The ACD (Automatic Call Distribution) engine evaluates the ticket's queue and routes it to the next available qualified agent using the tenant's configured routing strategy.

Routing strategies
Round-robin · Skills-based · Least-loaded · Priority-weighted
Eligibility check
Agent must be: active, online, assigned to the target queue, not at capacity
If no agent available
Ticket waits in queue. SLA breach timer ticks. Manager alerted at breach threshold.
Ticket field updated
assignee_id set to matched agent_id; status remains "open"

The assigned agent receives a notification (push / in-app) that a new ticket is waiting in their queue.

6

Agent Accepts the Ticket — TAT Clock Starts

The assigned agent opens the ticket and clicks "Accept". This is the critical handoff point that starts the Turn-Around Time (TAT) clock and changes the ticket status to "in-progress".

TAT start timestamp
Recorded at exact moment of acceptance — used for SLA compliance calculation
Status change
open → in-progress
Audit entry
status_changed: open → in-progress; actor = agent_id
SLA implication
SLA clock was already ticking from ticket creation (first response SLA). TAT is a separate agent-level metric starting here.

Before acceptance, the agent can see the ticket but cannot make changes. After acceptance, the agent owns the ticket and can update fields, add notes, and resolve.

7

Originator Gets View-Only Access + Note Capability

Once the ticket is accepted by an agent, the originator (the customer or person who raised the ticket, granted the "Viewer" role) is automatically notified and given specific access to the ticket.

What originator can see
Ticket status · Subject · Public replies from agent · Their own notes
What originator CANNOT see
Internal notes · Audit log · Assignee details · Priority/queue configuration
What originator can DO
Add notes / comments for updates (visible to agent and manager)
Access gate
Ticket must be in "in-progress" or later state. No access during "open" pre-acceptance state.

This design follows the principle of least privilege: the customer can track progress and add context without being able to interfere with the ticket workflow.

8

Agent Works the Ticket — Supervisor Can Override

The agent works toward resolution. During this phase, multiple governance guardrails apply if supervisors need to intervene.

Agent actions
Update fields · Add public/internal notes · Move to resolved
Manager intervention — Priority change
Manager can change priority BUT must provide a written reason. Logged as priority_changed audit entry.
Manager intervention — Reassignment
Manager can reassign to another agent (emergency reroute). Optional written reason. Logged as assignee_changed audit entry.
TAT visibility
Manager sees live TAT elapsed time. Breach alerts trigger at configured thresholds.
9

Ticket Resolved and Closed

Agent marks ticket as "resolved". After a configured hold period (or immediate close), the ticket moves to "closed" status. TAT clock stops at resolution timestamp.

TAT stop timestamp
Recorded at resolution. Final TAT = accept_time → resolve_time.
Status flow
in-progress → resolved → closed
Originator notified
Notification sent (email / push) that their ticket has been resolved
Post-close: tenant_admin only
Only tenant_admin can hard-delete a closed ticket. All other roles: no delete access at any stage.

CSAT (Customer Satisfaction) survey may be triggered to the originator after closure, if configured by tenant_admin.
